2 · Add applications

Import a CSV with columns name, availability (availability = noon, evening, or both), or paste lines like PlayerName, both. Names not in the roster are added automatically as non-guaranteed. Applicants are placed into teams below immediately — each name appears once, in a team, on the waitlist, or in the banned list. If teams are already formed, you'll be asked whether late additions re-shuffle everyone or just join the waitlist.

3 · Teams

Selection order: guaranteed players first, then everyone else by longest time since last selection for this mode (never-selected players first; ties broken randomly). Players banned for a recent no-show in this mode are excluded (Desert and Canyon bans are separate). Availability only decides which team you join, never whether you get in — noon-only and both-slot applicants have equal odds. Edits are manual: ↓ drops to waitlist, ↑ promotes, ✕ removes from the event — nothing reshuffles on its own. To change someone's availability, remove them and re-add with the new slot.

Alliance roster

Guaranteed players always get a spot when they apply (unless banned for a no-show). Bans are automatic and per mode: a no-show blocks selection in that game mode only (Desert or Canyon) for 14 days from the event date. If a player changes their in-game name, use ✏️ to rename them — all history, bans and fairness priority stay attached, and applications under the old name still match automatically. If the same person ended up as two roster entries (typo, different spelling), use 🔗 to merge the duplicate into the real one — histories are combined and the duplicate name becomes an alias.

How selection works

1. Banned players (no-show within the ban window for this mode) are removed from the pool. Desert Storm and Canyon Storm bans are tracked separately — a no-show in one doesn't block the other.
2. Guaranteed applicants fill spots first. If more guaranteed players apply than there are spots, the least-recently-picked among them win.
3. Remaining spots go to non-guaranteed applicants ordered by longest time since last selection in that mode. Never-selected players come first. Ties are broken randomly.
4. Noon-only players go to the Noon team, evening-only to Evening, and "both" players fill whichever team has more space — availability never changes your odds of getting in.
5. Within each team, spots fill in selection order: the first N picked become starters, the rest substitutes. You can toggle these before saving.
6. Unselected applicants form a ranked waitlist for drop-out replacements.
